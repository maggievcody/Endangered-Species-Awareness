There are many reasons species become endangered.

The following are some of the primary reasons.

Habitat Loss

Habitat is essential to a species. Without ecological stability in their habitat, a species is more likely to become threatened.

Over Hunting

An unfortunately large number of species have been hunted beyond sustainable levels and now are threatened with extinction.

Environmental Factors

Changes in the environment are a large contribution to species endangerement. Climate change is a large contributor to this.

Human Development

Humans are responsible for clearing many areas for agriculture and transportation that are destructive to wildlife habitat.

Competition

Competition can contribute to endangered species when a species needs to compete for resources such as food or land.

Disease

Small populations, especially those limited in terms of diversity are especially vulnerable to disease.
